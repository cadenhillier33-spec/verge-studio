Handle Every Objection

"$1,500 is too much"
"How much is one new customer worth to you? If you're a restaurant, one table a night is $50-$100. That's $1,500 in a month. The site pays for itself in 30 days."
"I'll just use Wix"
"Be honest — how long has that been on your to-do list? Most owners try Wix, spend 20 hours fighting the editor, and end up with a template. Your time is worth more than that."
"I need to think about it"
"Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think over — is it the price, the timing, or something else?"

↑ Forces them to name the real objection. Then handle THAT.

"I already have a website"
"I saw it — and respectfully, it's not doing what it should. Pull it up on your phone right now. Is that the first impression you want to give customers?"
"My nephew can do it free"
"Ha — I hear that a lot. Usually it takes 6 months, looks amateur, and then they're too busy to fix anything. Your business is your livelihood — it's too important to leave to a favor."
"Business is slow right now"
"That's exactly why you need this. When business is slow, you need more customers finding you. A website works 24/7 — it's a salesperson that never sleeps."
"I don't think a website will help"
"97% of people search online before visiting a local business. If you're not there, you literally don't exist to them. Your competitors are showing up — every customer that finds them instead of you is money you're leaving on the table."
